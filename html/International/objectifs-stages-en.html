The internship constitutes an important part of the student's training. This first contact with the reality of the profession should enable him to synthesize the knowledge acquired at the IUT, to become aware of the socio-professional environment and to specify his personal aptitudes.

The subject of the internship must be identified by the company and validated by the department after consultation. During the course of the internship, the department ensures internship follow-up requiring information exchanges between the company and the department. This monitoring should be carried out, as far as possible, by visits by teachers to the place of the internship.

At the end of the internship, the student must submit an internship report to a jury comprising the internship supervisor in the company, the tutor tutor of the intern and another permanent teacher in the department. This report and this defense are formal in nature and each gives rise to a qualitative and quantitative evaluation.

The internship is evaluated jointly by the company / organization (company / organization tutor) and the department (teacher tutor and jury) on the following elements:

  • Work in a company / organization, with regard to the objectives set in the agreement
  • The written report, framed in its form, highlighting the skills implemented during the internship
  • Oral defense by a mixed company / organization, department jury

For these three elements, the trainee's evaluation must relate to:

  • Its ability to use its academic achievements in carrying out its mission
  • The skills resulting from immersion in the professional environment: technical skills and interpersonal skills with reference to the reference of activities and skills of the IT DUT
